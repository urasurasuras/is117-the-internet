Packets, Routing, and Reliability

Information on the Internet goes around in packets, and in a very indirect fashion.
Packets travel a lot like how you would travel in traffic. Depending on traffic conjestions or road conditions, it might change paths.
Data is divided into packets so that each packet can take a different path to the same destination and get "reassembled" at the end, into its original state.
Each packet has their source and destination address, and their route is determined by devices called "routers".

For example, Spotify sends packets using TCP, meaning that packets of the audio stream will be sent but individual packets will always be played in the correct order.
